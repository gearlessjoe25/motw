The Banyan Tribe is a lineage of soul-bound hunters and scholars originating in West Africa. Renowned for crafting weapons from monster parts and manipulating souls, they have historically acted as guardians of their land, knowledge, and people.

Origins

Panin-Anasi I founded the tribe, using monster parts to protect his village and create powerful weapons. He was appointed chief for his courage and good heart, uniting neighboring villages through marriage and diplomacy.

Over centuries, the tribe spread throughout Sub-Saharan and Western Africa, refining their soul-based techniques, weaponry, and leadership. Key figures such as Asantewaa developed the Legacy Weapons, while Kwabena and Kwame preserved freedom and heritage in West Africa.

The Banyan Tribe emphasizes responsibility, mastery of soul powers, and protection of community. Rituals, crafting of artifacts from monster parts, and soul-attunement ceremonies are central to their identity. Marks on the body allow manipulation of souls and attunement to weapons.
